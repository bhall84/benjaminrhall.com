What you’ll walk away with

You won’t leave as an AI expert. But you’ll leave knowing what AI actually does, where to be cautious, and how to start using it for things you care about — on your terms.

The hesitation many older adults feel about AI isn’t ignorance — it’s good judgment. What you need isn’t more hype. It’s:

A safe space to ask “dumb” questions — there aren’t any
Hands-on practice with someone patient nearby — not a YouTube video
Clear first steps that feel manageable — not a firehose of information
Permission to be skeptical — because healthy skepticism about AI is a feature, not a bug

Common Questions

You might be wondering…

Do I need any AI experience?

Not at all. About one-third of participants in my recent workshop had never used AI before. We start from the very beginning.

Will I become an AI expert?

No — and I don't claim you will. What you'll gain is enough confidence and knowledge to start experimenting on your own. Think of it as an orientation, not a certification. And healthy skepticism about AI is part of being AI-literate — not an obstacle to it.

What if I still feel confused after the workshop?

That's normal. The goal is progress, not perfection. Many people book 1-on-1 coaching sessions or join a follow-up practice lab to get additional help.

How is this different from free resources?

Free resources can't answer your specific questions, troubleshoot your device, or create a supportive environment where mistakes are expected. I provide all of that.

What about scams and AI-generated misinformation?

We discuss common tactics that scammers use to target older adults and look at examples of AI-generated content. We also cover how AI tools themselves can produce inaccurate information — sometimes called "hallucinations" — so it's not just about bad actors using AI, but about the tools' own limitations. For deeper scam defense training, I recommend working with cybersecurity or consumer protection specialists — that's a different area of expertise than what I offer.

I'm not very good with computers. Is this still for me?

If you can send an email and use a web browser, you have the skills you need. I specialize in working with people who don't consider themselves "tech people."

What equipment do I need?

For online sessions, just a computer, tablet, or phone with an internet connection — we’ll meet over Google Meet. For in-home coaching sessions, all you need is your own device. I’ll come to you and we’ll work side by side at your desk or kitchen table. For in-person group workshops, I bring everything we need.

I represent a library or senior center. Can you work with our group?

Yes. I offer group workshops for organizations and can also develop a complete curriculum for your staff to deliver. Learn more about workshop options or explore curriculum development.

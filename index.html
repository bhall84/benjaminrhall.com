My Approach

Learning AI should feel like learning from a trusted friend

Every session is designed around how adults actually learn — with real-world relevance, plenty of practice and repetition, and zero jargon.

1

We start with what matters to you

Before our first session, I'll learn about your goals, your comfort level, and the things you'd love AI to help with. Planning trips? Writing letters? Organizing photos? Your interests drive everything we do.

2

Learn by doing, at your pace

Every session is hands-on. You'll use AI tools in real time, with me right there guiding you. We go at your speed, repeat as needed, and celebrate small wins along the way. No rush, no pressure.

3

Build confidence that sticks

I design each session to build on the last, using proven adult learning principles. You'll develop real confidence gradually, with each new skill reinforcing what you've already practiced.

4

Take it with you

After each session, you'll receive printed guides, checklists, and prompt cheat-sheets — simple reference materials you can keep by your computer. No need to memorize anything.

Common Questions

You might be wondering…

Do I need any technical experience?

Not at all. If you can send an email and browse the internet, you have everything you need to get started. My training is specifically designed for people who aren't "tech people" — and that's exactly my specialty.

What equipment do I need?

Just a computer (Mac or PC) with an internet connection. Sessions are conducted over Zoom video call, and we'll use web-based AI tools that don't require any special software. I can also meet in person if you're local.

How are sessions different from a YouTube tutorial?

Tutorials are one-size-fits-all. My sessions are built entirely around you — your goals, your pace, your questions. It's like having a patient, knowledgeable friend sitting next to you, guiding you through each step and making sure you truly understand before moving on.

What if I need to go slower or repeat something?

That's not just okay — it's expected and welcomed. Repetition is how adults build lasting skills. I design sessions with plenty of practice time, and you'll always have printed guides to refer back to on your own.

What's the difference between the online course and the 1-on-1 packages?

The self-paced course lets you learn independently, on your own schedule, with built-in knowledge checks and downloadable guides. The 1-on-1 packages give you personalized, live guidance tailored to your specific goals. Some people start with the course and then add 1-on-1 sessions for deeper help — whatever works best for you.

I'm not sure which option is right for me.

That's exactly what the free consultation is for. We'll talk about your goals and I'll recommend the best fit — whether that's the self-paced course, one of the training packages, or a combination. Many people start with the AI Starter Assessment to get a clear roadmap.

Is AI safe? I've heard concerns about privacy.

Great question, and one I take very seriously. Privacy, safety, and ethical use are woven into every session. I'll show you exactly what AI can and can't do, how to protect your personal information, and how to use these tools responsibly. You'll feel informed, not just capable.

I represent a library or senior center. Can you train our community?

Absolutely. I design complete "AI Literacy for Older Adults" curricula for organizations — with facilitator guides, handouts, slide decks, and evaluation tools. Learn more about organizational packages or reach out directly.
